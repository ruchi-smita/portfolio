PROCESS

From Raw Material To Monumental Artistry

A quiet progression from first thought to final placement, shaped through hand, material, shadow, and time.

01 Concept & Sketching

Every commission begins as a study of place, proportion, and emotional weight. Early forms are explored through sketches, clay studies, and quiet observation.

02 Material Selection

Stone, clay, metal, and casting surfaces are selected for their density, grain, patina, and permanence. The material is chosen to carry the story before it is shaped.

03 Sculpting & Formation

The form is built through pressure, subtraction, joining, and instinct. Surfaces begin to hold gesture, anatomy, rhythm, and the visible evidence of the hand.

04 Refinement & Detailing

Texture, contour, ornament, and light are refined until the work gains depth. Details are composed to reward both distant viewing and intimate inspection.

05 Final Installation

The completed piece is placed in conversation with architecture, landscape, and light, becoming a permanent presence within its commissioned environment.
